Medical and Dental College Admission Test (MDCAT)

What is MDCAT?

The Medical and Dental College Admission Test (MDCAT) is a standardized entrance exam required for admission to medical and dental colleges in Pakistan. Conducted by the Pakistan Medical Commission (PMC), MDCAT evaluates a candidate's knowledge and aptitude in subjects such as Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and English, along with logical reasoning skills.

Key Details About MDCAT:

  • Frequency: MDCAT is conducted annually, typically in August or September.
  • Eligibility: Students with at least 65% marks in FSc (Pre-Medical) or equivalent are eligible to appear.
  • Test Type: Computer-based or manual test, depending on the PMC's arrangements.
  • Duration: 3 hours and 30 minutes.
  • Total Marks: 200 marks.

MDCAT Syllabus

The MDCAT syllabus is designed to test candidates' command over concepts from the FSc Pre-Medical curriculum. Here's a breakdown of the subjects and topics:

  • Biology: Cell biology, human physiology, genetics, plant physiology, and more.
  • Chemistry: Organic, inorganic, and physical chemistry.
  • Physics: Mechanics, thermodynamics, waves, and modern physics.
  • English: Grammar, comprehension, and vocabulary.
  • Logical Reasoning: Analytical and problem-solving skills.

How to Prepare for MDCAT?

Preparing for MDCAT requires a strategic approach. Follow these tips to maximize your chances of success:

  • Study the official MDCAT syllabus thoroughly.
  • Practice past papers to familiarize yourself with the question format.
  • Take mock tests to improve time management.
  • Focus on weak areas by revisiting challenging topics.
  • Use online resources and study groups for collaborative learning.
